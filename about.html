Origin

Anniston,
Alabama

Anniston is not a city that makes the maps people celebrate. It sits in Calhoun County, Alabama, in the foothills of the Appalachians — a place with deep industrial history, a contamination legacy most would rather forget, and the kind of quiet that forces a person to go inward or go nowhere.

Fort McClellan is there. It carries its own history — one that touches his bloodline in ways that took years to fully understand. The soil, the exposure, the silence around it. That origin context isn't incidental. It's foundational.

The number 256 is the Alabama area code. It is also 2⁸ — the first byte, the base unit of digital information, a threshold. It's tattooed on his jaw. Some numbers choose you before you understand why.

"I was born in the kind of place that tells you what you are. I decided to find out for myself."

The Self-Education

Sixteen Years
of Free Study

Left formal schooling early. Earned a GED at 16. Then spent the next sixteen-plus years reading, building frameworks, and developing a pattern of independent research that most institutions don't have a name for.

The domains: consciousness studies, pattern recognition, sacred geometry, ancient wisdom traditions, linguistics, organizational psychology, cognitive architecture, AI theory. Not as separate subjects. As interlocking systems that point at the same underlying territory.

What emerged was a mind trained on synthesis rather than specialization — capable of holding contradictions, detecting signal in noise, and finding the structural skeleton beneath surface complexity. That's not a credential. It's a capability. There's a difference.
